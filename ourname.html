Our name Gala’s table was born from the belief that art and the table and the spaces between have the capacity to build a better world.

The company’s name was inspired by Les Diners de Gala, a “cookbook” created by the Spanish Surrealist artist Salvador Dali and originally published in 1973. More than a cookbook, this artist book and archive lives on as a layered document displaying Dali’s expansive imagination, and providing a window into the lives of Dali and Gala, his wife and life-long muse.

The surrealist dinner parties that Dali and Gala hosted were manifestations of the creative process, where performance, experimentation and a diverse array of food, textures, colors, and collaborations were brought together and celebrated at the table. The table as a catalyst for creativity, innovation and connection is what inspired us.
